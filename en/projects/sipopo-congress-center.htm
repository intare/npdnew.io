Founded by the Honorary President Mr. Mete Bora and presently managed by the Chairman of the Board of Directors Mr. Selim Bora and board member, CEO Mr. Fatih Bora, Summa Turizm Yatirimciligi A.S. was established in 1989 in Ankara to undertake international construction projects as General Contractor. The company started its construction activities abroad in 1990 with "Gara Altin” Presidential Guest House Project in Ashgabat, the capital of Turkmenistan, which was completed in less than 11 months period. Throughout 21 years of its operation, Summa has successfully completed more than 70 turn-key projects in 5 different countries and delivered more than 1 million m2 of closed area. 

Being currently active in Russia, Libya, Romania, Moldova and Turkey in construction, engineering and investment fields, Summa has gained international recognition especially in construction of administrative buildings, housing projects, hospitals, hotels, shopping malls, airports, stadiums, congress halls and high-rise buildings. As a consequence the company ranks among the "Top 225 International Contractors” according to the "Engineering News-Record” Magazine for the last 6 years.

Summa employs more than 3000 employees including more than 150 engineers and executive personnel in the countries of its operation and their construction sites. Operating in compliance with international standards both in headquarters, representative offices abroad and construction sites, Summa is an international construction company, which takes part in ambitious projects and possesses ISO 9001-2000 Quality Management System Certificate.

Customers always appreciated the Company due to its successful operation in harsh geographical and climatic conditions. Some of the examples are "Tuymaada Olympic Stadium” constructed in the record period of 9 months in Siberia in 50 degrees below zero Celsius, "Bulayla Twin Towers”, the tallest building in Northern Africa, constructed in Libya in 40 degrees above zero Celsius and the Headquarters of UralSibBank, the tallest building in the Ural region. Moreover, the "Festival Shopping Mall” project constructed in Moscow received the Most Popular Shopping Mall award in the year 2006, whereas the "Emerald Residences” project in the capital of Romania, Bucharest, received the Best Residential Development award in the year 2009.

Summa also acts as an investor and develops several real estate projects, hotel, hospital and shopping mall in Romania, Moldova and Libya. Moreover, the company became an example model of cooperation with international financial institutions in this field. At the moment Summa is working on the investment project of construction of 90 MW capacity Wind Farm, as an alternative renewable energy source.

INTRODUCTION

It all began on 29th of November 2010 on a shiny day of Tripoli, on the Terrace of Radisson Blu Al Mahary Hotel in Libya.

Me and Mr. Onur Çetinceviz were looking at the beautiful bay area from the 15th floor of the hotel when the General Manager Mr. Chauve stepped in and said that the President of Equatorial Guinea wants to meet with the owner of the hotel.

We noticed that His Excellency Teodoro Obiang Nguema Mbasogo was our guest who just checked-in to the Presidential Suite in order to participate African Union Summit 2010 with his delegation.

We asked from the head of his protocol Mr. Evuna for a proper time to see him during his stay with us. The same day he gave us an appointment at 10:00 pm at the executive lounge of the hotel where we had the pleasure to meet him in person for the first time.

The meeting which took about one hour gave us the opportunity to present both companies NPD and ONUR to His Excellency .

During the meeting I mentioned to His Excellency President about Hotel, Towers, and other construction projects we have completed in Libya by showing him from the terrace of the hotel while he was mentioning about the satisfaction and the good words he heard from Libyan officials about NPD for quality and speed of construction projects the company undertook so far.

We mentioned that although ONUR does not work in Libya, it is a worldwide known road and Infrastructure Company capable of executing fast track projects.

At the end of the meeting the President asked both companies to visit his home country as soon as possible to discuss construction project of a conference halland airport infrastructure whereas both projects were supposed to be done in fast track manner.

After having received such an invitation from thehighest level of a State, we did not even think twice and accepted immediately the invitation to make this first visit which demonstrated typical reflex shown by Turkish contractors which later on turned into a world record fast track design and build construction project from scratch.

On the 6th of December exactly 1 week after our meeting in Tripoli, Me, Mr. Onur Cetinceviz, Mr. Hakan Caka, Mr. Faruk Yıldırım and Mrs. Melkan Tabanlioglu flew to Malabo, Capital of Equatorial Guinea to find out the details about what was needed to be done. On the same day we were asked to visit the job site together with the President.

The moment we were at job site we realized that His Excellency President had concerns about the design and capacity of the existing facilities thus wanted us to tell him what kind of a more proper annex building we could build in 5 months.

He gave us only two parameters;

a. To build a conference hall in such a way that it could be capable to host 60 Heads of States and 5 delegates of each.

b. To finish the building on turn-key basis by the 5th of June 2011.

The next day we returned back to Turkey in order to prepare a concept design for further approval of His Excellency President who asked to meet again on 15th of December 2010.

In 9 days we completed the concept drawings and flew back to Malabo on 16th of December the date when His Excellency President approved all the architectural design works completely.

Then we started thinking about how to build such a huge conference center without having any idea about availability of local goods and services.

Then we found out that the availability of local goods and services necessary to build this project were limited (considering the time constraint we had while we were promising H.E. President) to deliver the project on time with desired quality.

We realized the complexity of the logistics andtook immediate measures to catch the deadline. All departments worked in harmony. 1600 tons of structural steel has been manufactured in a record short timeand shipped to job site for further installation. No need to mention about all other disciplines doing the same where otherwise would be impossible to reach this result.

Taking this opportunity, on behalf of both companies NPD and ONUR, I would like to thank all our managers, engineers, workers, subcontractors, material suppliers, logistic companies, local authorities and staff of central office in Istanbul.

Thanks to H.E. President Obiang for his trust in ourselves and in our companies.
